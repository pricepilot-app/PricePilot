About the project

Built by an independent creator who wanted to solve a real problem.

PricePilot started as a question: why are dropshippers still guessing their listing price? This is the answer.

My name is Yash. I built PricePilot because I kept seeing the same problem: dropshippers copying competitors' prices, adjusting by gut feel, and never really knowing if they were leaving margin on the table or pricing themselves out of sales.

The insight is simple: the relationship between price and demand isn't linear. It's a curve. If you price too low, you sell more but earn less per unit. If you price too high, volume collapses. Somewhere in the middle is a point where (price − cost) × units sold is maximized. That's the optimal price — and finding it requires math, not intuition.

PricePilot fits a quadratic regression model to your own (price, units sold) pairs to estimate your demand curve, then sweeps candidate prices across your observed range to find the peak. Every recommendation comes with a confidence score based on how strong the signal in your data is, so you always know when to trust it and when to log more batches first.

Your data is stored securely in Google Firebase. This ensures your sales history is private, encrypted, and available across any device you use to sign in.

This is still early. The model has real limitations: it needs price variation across your entries to work well, it only operates within your observed price range, and the recommendation is clamped to a safety band based on your observed prices (mean ± 2 standard deviations) to avoid wild swings. I'm being upfront about all of that because I'd rather you use it knowing exactly what it is than be misled by inflated claims.

If this ends up helping even a few people price smarter, that's the whole point.

Algorithm

Quadratic regression via 3×3 determinant. Pearson correlation for confidence scoring.

Stack

Vanilla HTML, CSS, JavaScript. Zero frameworks. Chart.js for visualizations.

Auth

Firebase Authentication for Google sign-in and email/password accounts.

— Yash, 2026
