Questions

Straight answers. No fluff.

How much data do I need before it works?
At least 3 entries where your selling price varied across batches and at least 15 total units sold across those entries. More price variation improves the demand curve fit — and raises the confidence score. If you've always sold at the same price, the model can't work.
What exactly do I enter?
Three numbers per batch: total revenue earned, total fulfillment cost paid, and how many units you sold. The app derives your average selling price and weighted unit cost automatically.
What does the confidence score mean?
It's based on the Pearson correlation between your price and quantity data. High means the signal in your data is strong and the recommendation is reliable. Low means the data is noisy or flat — add more entries before acting on the recommendation.
Can I track multiple products?
Yes. Create a separate product in the hub for each SKU. Each one gets an independent data history, demand model, and recommendation. No data bleeds between products.
Does the optimizer account for my costs?
Yes. The optimization sweeps candidate prices using (price − weighted average unit cost) × predicted demand, so you're maximizing profit margin, not just revenue.
Where is my data stored?
Everything is stored securely in Google Firebase. Your data is private, encrypted, and accessible only by you across any device.
Is it really free?
Yes, completely free. Sign up with email or Google — no credit card, no trial period, no catch.
What are the model's limitations?
Three main ones: it needs price variation in your entries, it only operates within the price range you've actually tested, and the recommendation is clamped to a safety band (mean ± 2 standard deviations) of your average to prevent wild swings. Full transparency here matters.
